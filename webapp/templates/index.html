What Cell Painting measures. Cell Painting is a microscopy assay that stains cells with multiple fluorescent dyes marking different structures — nucleus, endoplasmic reticulum, RNA, the cytoskeleton/Golgi/membrane, and mitochondria. Imaging all of these together captures a detailed snapshot of a cell's morphology after treatment with a compound.
What the model learned. A self-supervised vision model (DINO) was trained on these images without any MoA labels. It learned to compress each image into a 384-number vector — an embedding — such that images showing visually similar cell morphology end up with similar vectors.
How to read similarity. "Similarity" here means cosine similarity between embeddings — a measure of shared visual phenotype, not a direct measure of shared mechanism. Compounds with similar phenotypes sometimes share a biological pathway, but similarity is a hypothesis to investigate, not a confirmed mechanism.
