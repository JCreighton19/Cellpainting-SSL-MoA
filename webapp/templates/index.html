About this visualization

What does each point represent?

Each point is one well. Its position comes from a 2D projection (UMAP) of the 384-number embedding a self-supervised model (DINO) produced for that well's cell images.

What does distance mean?

Nearby points correspond to phenotypically similar perturbations in the learned embedding space. Distances suggest similarity but should not be interpreted as causal biological relationships.

Why are only some mechanisms colored?

Only the {{ stats.n_moas_in_legend }} most frequent mechanisms get their own color (covering {{ "%.0f"|format(stats.pct_top10_coverage * 100) }}% of wells) — another {{ "%.0f"|format(stats.pct_other_annotated * 100) }}% of wells do have an annotated mechanism but are grouped as "other" purely for legend readability; only {{ "%.0f"|format(stats.pct_unannotated * 100) }}% have no annotation at all.

Embeddings and similarity

Similarity between two wells is the cosine similarity of their postprocessed DINO embeddings — a measure of shared visual phenotype, not a direct measure of shared biological mechanism.
